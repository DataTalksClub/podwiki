A data product manager turns data work into a product with users, priorities,
success metrics, and an adoption path. The role sits between business,
engineering, analytics, data science, and platform teams.

In the DataTalks.Club podcast archive, data product management is not just
dashboard management. It is a discipline for deciding which data work should
exist and how it should create value.

## Core Responsibilities

- understand user and stakeholder problems
- define metrics and semantic meaning
- prioritize by impact, effort, cost, and risk
- translate between business and technical teams
- guide discovery, delivery, and rollout
- make adoption measurable
- keep feedback loops alive after launch

## Product Manager vs Product Owner

Podcast guests distinguish the two roles. A product manager usually owns the
broader problem, strategy, and user value. A product owner often works closer to
delivery execution and team prioritization. In data work, companies often blur
these roles, which is why explicit ownership matters.
