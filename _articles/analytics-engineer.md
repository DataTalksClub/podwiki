An analytics engineer builds trusted analytical data models for analysts,
business users, product teams, and data scientists. The role sits between data
engineering and analytics: more engineering discipline than a traditional
analyst role, but closer to business questions than many platform engineering
roles.

In the DataTalks.Club archive, analytics engineering is not just "SQL plus
dashboards." It is the work of making raw data usable: modeling, testing,
documenting, transforming, and exposing data so people can make decisions
without rewriting the same cleanup logic.

## What Analytics Engineers Do

Analytics engineers usually work on:

- SQL transformations and data models
- dbt projects, tests, documentation, and DAGs
- dimensional modeling and semantic definitions
- data quality checks and schema-change handling
- BI layers such as Looker or similar tools
- collaboration with analysts, data scientists, and backend engineers
- debugging broken reports, late data, and inconsistent metrics

The output is not only a dashboard. The output is a reliable analytical layer
that many people can reuse.

## Analytics Engineer vs Data Analyst vs Data Engineer

A data analyst usually focuses on analysis, reporting, experimentation, and
business interpretation. A data engineer usually focuses on ingestion,
infrastructure, orchestration, storage, and platform reliability.

An analytics engineer lives in the middle. They understand the business context,
but they apply software practices: version control, testing, modular modeling,
documentation, and deployable transformation workflows.

## Skills to Prioritize

Start with SQL, data modeling, and business context. Then add dbt, testing,
warehouse basics, Git, BI modeling, orchestration awareness, and communication.

For career switchers, the archive suggests a pragmatic path: move from Excel or
domain reporting into SQL, build dashboards, contribute to small internal data
projects, and use domain knowledge as an advantage rather than hiding it.
