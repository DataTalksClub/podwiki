A data scientist interview tests more than algorithms. It tests whether you can
frame business problems, reason about data, write enough code and SQL, evaluate
models, explain trade-offs, and communicate impact.

The DataTalks.Club archive includes perspectives from recruiters, hiring
managers, and data science leaders. Their advice converges on one point:
preparation should match the role. A product data scientist, machine learning
engineer, research scientist, and analytics-heavy data scientist will be
evaluated differently.

## What to Prepare

Prepare in five layers:

1. A concise story about your background and target role.
2. A CV that shows personal contribution, not only tools.
3. SQL, coding, statistics, and machine learning fundamentals.
4. Case-study narratives that connect business goals to metrics.
5. Clear examples of projects, trade-offs, failures, and lessons learned.

## Typical Interview Stages

The archive describes a common funnel: recruiter screen, intro conversation,
technical round, take-home or live exercise, case study, behavioral interviews,
and offer discussion. Not every company uses every step, but most interviews
combine role fit, technical depth, communication, and practical judgment.

Use recruiter conversations to clarify the depth expected. If the role is
product analytics-heavy, prioritize SQL, experimentation, metrics, and
stakeholder scenarios. If the role is ML engineering-heavy, expect more system
design, model evaluation, coding, and deployment discussion.

## Common Mistake

The common mistake is practicing isolated questions without preparing the story
around your work. Interviewers need evidence that you can turn ambiguous
problems into useful analysis or systems. Practice explaining what you built,
why it mattered, what you measured, and what you would change next time.
