# Building Production ML Platforms: Infrastructure, Workflows, Teams & Governance That Scale

## Why This Episode Matters

This is a high-value MLOps platform episode because it covers both the technical pieces and the organizational reasons for building them. It gives future agents vocabulary for experiment tracking, model registries, deployment patterns, metadata, lineage, compliance, and platform team design. It is especially useful when comparing build-versus-buy and incremental platform adoption.

## Chapter-Level Summary

- 1:14 - Episode Introduction: Sets up MLOps and ML platform building with Simon Stiebellehner.
- 2:00 - Career & Transition: Covers research-to-industry movement and early platform work.
- 4:42 - MLOps Definition: Defines MLOps as people, processes, and technology.
- 6:55 - Deployment Challenges: Explains early blockers that motivated platform work.
- 8:11 - Core Platform Skills: Lists cloud infrastructure, Kubernetes, and Terraform as key skills.
- 10:47 - User-Centric Platform Design: Centers platform design on data scientist workflows.
- 13:25 - Engineering Fundamentals: Emphasizes software engineering foundations for ML platforms.
- 13:50 - Team Composition: Compares specialist and generalist skill mixes.
- 15:34 - Team Size & On-Call: Covers staffing and operational support needs.
- 16:52 - Build vs Buy Decision: Discusses when building an ML platform is justified.
- 17:14 - Platform Triggers: Identifies standardization needs across teams as a platform signal.
- 20:04 - Single-Team Value: Shows how SaaS and incremental adoption can help smaller teams.
- 21:03 - Data Science Workflow: Walks from exploration through training and evaluation.
- 28:20 - Self-Service Compute: Discusses notebooks and managed compute provisioning.
- 29:41 - Experiment Tracking: Positions tracking as an early reproducibility win.
- 30:32 - Model Registry: Covers persisting models for downstream use.
- 31:15 - Deployment Patterns: Compares batch inference and online serving.
- 31:51 - Orchestration Choices: Looks at Airflow, pipelines, and production workflows.
- 34:01 - Tool Integration: Discusses stitching SaaS and open source into one platform.
- 35:26 - LLMs & Emerging Needs: Notes how LLM use changes platform requirements.
- 38:40 - Developer Experience: Favors thin abstractions over cloud providers.
- 39:54 - Regulatory Constraints: Covers fintech, security, and compliance pressure.
- 42:48 - Metadata & Lineage: Links artifact logging and tracking to reproducibility.
- 45:50 - Data Governance: Notes GDPR implications of logging and dataset storage.
- 47:08 - Business-First Strategy: Warns against heavy platform investment before model value exists.
- 49:19 - Parallelization Strategy: Suggests building minimal platform pieces alongside use cases.
- 51:41 - MLOps Skill Focus: Discusses how much model internals platform engineers need.
- 54:15 - API Design & Logging: Covers unified prediction schemas for monitoring and analytics.
- 57:32 - Learning Resources: Points to books, projects, and training.

- The question is focused on RAG or LLM agent product design.
