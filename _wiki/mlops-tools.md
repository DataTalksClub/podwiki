## Definition

MLOps tools help teams move machine learning work from experiments into
repeatable systems. In the podcast archive, the tool categories include
experiment tracking and model registries. Guests also discuss pipeline
orchestration and feature stores. Deployment systems, monitoring, and platform
templates appear in the same discussions.

## Common Tool Groups

[Building Production ML Platforms]({{ '/podcasts/building-production-ml-platform-and-mlops-team/' | relative_url }})
connects tools to team operating models. The episode covers training
pipelines and batch inference. It also covers monitoring and platform ownership.
[Feature Stores
for MLOps]({{ '/podcasts/mlops-feature-stores-feature-stores-feast-tecton/' | relative_url }})
puts feature stores next to dbt and Airflow. It also compares Spark, Feast,
and Tecton.

The [Human-Centered MLOps and Model Monitoring]({{ '/podcasts/human-centered-mlops-and-model-monitoring/' | relative_url }})
shows why monitoring tools need useful alerts and ownership, not only metrics.

## Guest Differences

Guests differ on build versus buy and on how much platform a team needs. Small
teams can often start with fewer moving parts. Larger teams need standard
training and serving paths. They also need tracking, monitoring, and recovery
paths. Use [ML Platforms]({{ '/wiki/ml-platforms/' | relative_url }}) for the
platform view and [MLOps]({{ '/wiki/mlops/' | relative_url }}) for the
operating model.
