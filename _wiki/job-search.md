Job search is the candidate-side work of choosing a data, analytics, ML, or AI
role and proving fit. It links
[[career-transitions-in-data|career transition]],
[[career growth]],
[[CV screening]], and
[[job descriptions]]. A good
search also tests whether the company has the team, data, and hiring path to
use the candidate well.

DataTalks.Club guests define job search more narrowly than "apply to many
jobs." [[person:sarahmestiri|Sarah Mestiri]] builds it
around goals and strategy in
[[podcast:job-search-strategy-in-tech-projects-skills-cv-networking|Tech Job Search Strategy]].
She also covers networking and CV work there.

[[person:lukewhipps|Luke Whipps]] adds the recruiter
view in
[[podcast:get-data-scientist-job|Land Data Scientist Roles]].
[[person:nicksingh|Nick Singh]] treats interview
success as a communication problem in
[[podcast:data-interview-behavioral-and-portfolio-prep-guide|Ace Data Interviews]].
Together, those episodes frame job search as a matching problem. Candidates
choose a role, build proof, explain that proof, and then evaluate the company.

## Targeting the Role

Role targeting starts by translating titles into work. Sarah asks candidates to
define the ideal role through tasks, skills, and future vision at 14:30 in
[[podcast:job-search-strategy-in-tech-projects-skills-cv-networking|Tech Job Search Strategy]].
At 15:07, she validates that choice with role analysis and informational
interviews. At 20:01, she separates interest from market demand so candidates
can pick a search direction before tailoring applications.

[[person:lindsaymcquade|Lindsay McQuade]] gives the
junior-candidate version at 20:28 and 23:48 in
[[podcast:get-junior-data-job-and-transferable-skills|Land Junior Data Jobs]].
The market blurs data scientist, analyst, and engineer titles, so a candidate
needs a job focus before rewriting a CV or portfolio. Her advice pairs well
with [[Data Scientist Role]],
[[Data Analyst Careers]],
and [[Data Engineer Role]]
when the title alone is too broad.

Data roles make this boundary important. [[person:olegnovikov|Oleg Novikov]]
separates product data science from machine learning engineering at 15:29 in
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]].
[[person:terezaiofciu|Tereza Iofciu]] warns at 20:06
and 23:01 in
[[podcast:data-science-job-red-flags-and-mismatched-roles|Data Science Jobs]]
that titles can hide the real work and team maturity. Candidates should read
[[Job Descriptions]] as evidence
about responsibilities, not as a tool wishlist.

## Search Breadth and Tailoring

Guests differ on how wide to search. [[person:jeffkatz|Jeff Katz]]
advises data engineering candidates at 15:53 in
[[podcast:get-data-engineering-job-prep-and-interview|Data Engineering Job Prep and Interview Guide]]
to apply broadly and avoid self-filtering too early. Luke and Sarah draw a
narrower boundary. Luke recommends target-company lists at 44:26 in
[[podcast:get-data-scientist-job|Land Data Scientist Roles]].
Sarah recommends segmentation and focused outreach at 29:35 in
[[podcast:job-search-strategy-in-tech-projects-skills-cv-networking|Tech Job Search Strategy]].

The disagreement isn't between mass applications and applying to five jobs.
Early candidates may need volume to learn the market. Guests still warn that
unfocused volume weakens CV tailoring, interview preparation, and networking.

Lindsay makes the middle path explicit for juniors at 30:37 in
[[podcast:get-junior-data-job-and-transferable-skills|Land Junior Data Jobs]].
She recommends enough applications to get market signal and enough tailoring to
learn from each attempt. That balance sits between [[career-transitions-in-data|career transition]]
and [[hiring]], because the candidate has
to learn how recruiters and teams describe fit.

## CV Evidence

Across the job-search episodes, the CV acts as a proof surface. Luke starts
with first impressions, formatting, and clarity at 14:07 in
[[podcast:get-data-scientist-job|Land Data Scientist Roles]].
At 19:50 and 25:04, he asks candidates to connect tools to projects, use cases,
and business impact. At 37:54, candidates research job needs and map the CV to
those needs instead of sending the same document everywhere.

Oleg gives a similar rule at 18:28 and 44:38 in
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]].
The CV should be easy to scan, foreground personal contribution, and remove
irrelevant personal details. At 25:51, he treats the CV like a landing page for
the next hiring step rather than a biography.

For junior and transition candidates, the CV has to translate prior work.
Lindsay focuses on transferable skills at 11:51-15:06 in
[[podcast:get-junior-data-job-and-transferable-skills|Land Junior Data Jobs]].
She moves candidates from responsibility lists toward achievement-based
evidence.

[[person:nicolasrassam|Nicolas Rassam]]
gives the data engineering version at 31:16 in
[[podcast:hiring-for-data-engineering-jobs-in-europe|Hiring Data Engineers in Europe]].
Candidates should show SQL and Python. They should also show the problem
solved and the outcome. That makes [[CV Screening]]
part of job search, not only an employer-side topic.

## Portfolio Proof

Portfolio work matters when it shows reasoning through a real problem. Sarah
argues at 26:28 in
[[podcast:job-search-strategy-in-tech-projects-skills-cv-networking|Tech Job Search Strategy]]
that projects validate skills better than course completion alone. Oleg opens
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]]
with the same idea at 2:42. A project can differentiate an application before
the interview when it shows a candidate's own decisions.

The expected project changes by role. Jeff recommends personal projects and
open-source contributions at 2:46 in
[[podcast:get-data-engineering-job-prep-and-interview|Data Engineering Job Prep and Interview Guide]].
At 2:22 he names clean code, useful names, and tests as part of the data
engineering signal. Nicolas adds at 54:25-55:53 in
[[podcast:hiring-for-data-engineering-jobs-in-europe|Hiring Data Engineers in Europe]]
that standout projects should be shareable and explainable.

Nick asks candidates to lead with ownership and impact at 25:13 and 27:50 in
[[podcast:data-interview-behavioral-and-portfolio-prep-guide|Ace Data Interviews]].
Public work becomes interview material when the candidate can explain
tradeoffs, metrics, ownership, and business context. For role-specific project
shapes, see
[[Open Source Portfolio Evidence]],
[[Data Engineering Portfolio Projects]],
and
[[Machine Learning Portfolio Projects]].

## Networking and Referrals

Networking in the job-search episodes is targeted research, not mass messaging.
Sarah recommends weak ties, referrals, informational interviews, and weekly
outreach at 31:40-41:17 in
[[podcast:job-search-strategy-in-tech-projects-skills-cv-networking|Tech Job Search Strategy]].
At 34:18, she makes outreach short and personalized. At 36:10, she asks about
day-to-day work and success factors. Those questions help candidates evaluate
the role while they build a referral path.

Nick gives the interview-prep version at 58:26-1:00:59 in
[[podcast:data-interview-behavioral-and-portfolio-prep-guide|Ace Data Interviews]].
Cold emails work better when they include project links and specific evidence.
Lindsay adds LinkedIn informational outreach at 58:30 in
[[podcast:get-junior-data-job-and-transferable-skills|Land Junior Data Jobs]],
which is especially useful for juniors who can't rely on recruiters.

## Interviews and Assessments

Interview preparation starts with the hiring path. Oleg outlines a common data
science funnel at 13:24 in
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]].
The sequence usually starts with a recruiter screen, then moves to take-home
work and later interview rounds. Jeff gives the
data engineering version at 7:46 in
[[podcast:get-data-engineering-job-prep-and-interview|Data Engineering Job Prep and Interview Guide]],
where SQL, Python, and take-home projects are common formats. Nicolas adds at
26:38 in
[[podcast:hiring-for-data-engineering-jobs-in-europe|Hiring Data Engineers in Europe]]
that assessment varies by level.

Guests also draw different boundaries around portfolio and assessment work.
Oleg treats take-home projects as a return-on-investment decision at 27:51 in
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]].
Tereza warns at 16:25 in
[[podcast:data-science-job-red-flags-and-mismatched-roles|Data Science Jobs]]
that take-home tasks can overburden candidates. Candidates should ask what the
assessment measures and how much unpaid work it requires.

The behavioral and case-interview episodes are communication heavy. Nick
explains at 8:58 and 13:20 in
[[podcast:data-interview-behavioral-and-portfolio-prep-guide|Ace Data Interviews]]
that interviewers need more than technical skill. He recommends grid planning
and STAR stories. At 44:27 and 45:30, he starts case and product-sense
interviews by clarifying goals and metrics. He then handles assumptions
explicitly.

The same structured reasoning appears in
[[Machine Learning System Design]],
where candidates explain requirements and baselines. They also explain metrics
and tradeoffs.

## Career Changers and Juniors

Career changers need a bridge story, not an apology. Sarah works with career
changers and return-to-work candidates in
[[podcast:job-search-strategy-in-tech-projects-skills-cv-networking|Tech Job Search Strategy]].
At 53:30, she advises candidates to lead with results and transferable skills.
Lindsay builds the junior version at 11:51-13:02 in
[[podcast:get-junior-data-job-and-transferable-skills|Land Junior Data Jobs]]:
past experience becomes recruiter-friendly evidence for data roles.

Guests also offer proof paths without commercial experience. Jeff names
nonprofits, paid projects, and internships at 39:49 in
[[podcast:get-data-engineering-job-prep-and-interview|Data Engineering Job Prep and Interview Guide]].
Oleg gives PhD and cold-start candidates a project, synthetic-data, and
blogging path at 45:46 in
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]].
For fuller transition routes, use
[[career-transitions-in-data|Career Transition]],
[[Career Transitions in Data]],
and [[Academia]].

## Data Engineering Search

Data engineering search is more concrete than generic "data" search. The role
can be tested through pipelines, code, SQL, and operational judgment. Jeff
names Python and SQL, Docker and Airflow, and warehouse work at 1:20-9:41 in
[[podcast:get-data-engineering-job-prep-and-interview|Data Engineering Job Prep and Interview Guide]].
He also names code quality and database concepts. At 21:56 and 37:49, he warns
that certificates shouldn't replace skill proof and fundamentals.

Nicolas adds the recruiter view in
[[podcast:hiring-for-data-engineering-jobs-in-europe|Hiring Data Engineers in Europe]].
Transferable experience from software and BI roles matters at 20:57.
Junior-to-senior expectations differ at 22:55. For career switchers, focused
skills plus projects matter at 30:39. This candidate-side view complements
[[Data Engineer Role]],
[[data-engineer-roadmap|Data Engineering Roadmap]],
[[Data Engineering Portfolio Projects]],
and [[Hiring]].

## Company Evaluation and Offers

Tereza gives the clearest candidate-side company checklist at 23:01, 30:20,
38:51, and 48:32 in
[[podcast:data-science-job-red-flags-and-mismatched-roles|Data Science Jobs]].
Candidates should ask about team structure, objectives, data maturity, and
responsibilities. They should also ask about salary transparency and whether
the company knows what it wants from the role. That makes job search a
two-sided fit check, not only an application funnel.

Offer and rejection handling also matter. Oleg discusses offer components,
market comparison, negotiation, and current-salary problems at 42:02 and 50:17
in
[[podcast:data-science-interview-and-cv-guide|Data Science Interview Guide]].
He recommends gracious replies to rejections at 49:10 because relationships can
matter later. Luke adds salary signals and recruiter trust at 52:22-1:02:07 in
[[podcast:get-data-scientist-job|Land Data Scientist Roles]],
including how transparent to be about other interviews. For the compensation
side of the same conversation, see
[[Salary Negotiation]].
