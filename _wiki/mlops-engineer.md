## Definition

An MLOps engineer works on the systems that let machine learning teams train,
deploy, monitor, and maintain models reliably. The archive treats this role as
part software engineering, part platform engineering, and part production ML
operations.

## Common Responsibilities

[MLOps at Scale]({{ '/podcasts/mlops-at-scale-reproducibility-adoption/' | relative_url }})
connects the role to reproducibility, adoption, and repeatable workflows.
[Building Production ML Platforms]({{ '/podcasts/building-production-ml-platform-and-mlops-team/' | relative_url }})
adds platform ownership. Teams need clear paths for training, deployment, and
batch inference. They also need monitoring and support. The [Human-Centered MLOps
and Model Monitoring]({{ '/podcasts/human-centered-mlops-and-model-monitoring/' | relative_url }})
shows why the role also needs alert design, ownership, and collaboration with
model builders.

## Boundaries

MLOps engineers overlap with machine learning engineers, data engineers, and
platform engineers. Guests disagree less about the title than about where the
boundary sits in each company. Some teams expect model development too. Others
focus the role on infrastructure and tooling. They also expect CI/CD,
monitoring, and production support.
