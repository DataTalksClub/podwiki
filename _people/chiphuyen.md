# Chip Huyen

Chip Huyen is a writer, computer scientist, and author of Designing Machine Learning Systems. Her DataTalks.Club profile also notes her work on real-time machine learning platforms. She previously built ML tooling at NVIDIA and several other companies, including Snorkel AI, Netflix, and Primer. She teaches Stanford CS 329S on [machine learning system design]({{ '/wiki/machine-learning-system-design/' | relative_url }}).

DataTalks.Club hasn't published a local podcast interview with Chip. Guests still use her books when they discuss [production]({{ '/wiki/production/' | relative_url }}), [MLOps]({{ '/wiki/mlops/' | relative_url }}), and [AI engineering]({{ '/wiki/ai-engineering/' | relative_url }}).

## ML Systems Design for Production Work

In [Freelance Data Scientist Playbook](https://datatalks.club/podcast/from-startup-engineering-to-freelance-data-science.html), [Antonis Stellas](https://datatalks.club/people/antonisstellas.html) recommends Chip's Designing Machine Learning Systems at 58:11. He presents it as a practical resource for data scientists, machine learning engineers, and analysts. The recommendation follows his discussion of startup-style work and client projects. It also follows his discussion of [MLOps]({{ '/wiki/mlops/' | relative_url }}) and [model monitoring]({{ '/wiki/model-monitoring/' | relative_url }}).

Antonis doesn't mention the book as a theory text. He mentions it alongside The Lean Startup and Lean Analytics after discussing build-measure-learn work, measurement, and production constraints. Earlier in the same episode, he describes data drift, concept drift, and Evidently AI at 21:00.

He then describes a course project using MLflow, Prefect, and Grafana at 25:12. In that context, Chip's book supports a practical path for designing ML systems around deployment and monitoring. It also keeps operational feedback in view, not only model training.

## AI Engineering Beyond API Calls

In [From Medicine to Machine Learning](https://datatalks.club/podcast/nonlinear-path-to-machine-learning-freelancing-and-public-learning.html), [Pastor Soto](https://datatalks.club/people/pastorsoto.html) describes posting a quote from Chip's AI Engineering book at 36:33. The quote defines AI engineering as building on top of foundation models. The conversation then tests the definition. If someone reduces AI engineering to calling an API, Pastor says they miss the work needed to make a system function reliably.

Other DataTalks.Club AI engineering pages make the same distinction between [AI engineering]({{ '/wiki/ai-engineering/' | relative_url }}) and prompt-only work. The role includes application code and context management. It also includes evaluation, deployment, and operating the product after launch. For learners, the same thread leads to the [AI Engineering Roadmap]({{ '/wiki/ai-engineering-roadmap/' | relative_url }}), [LLM Evaluation Workflows]({{ '/wiki/llm-evaluation-workflows/' | relative_url }}), and [LLM Production Patterns]({{ '/wiki/llm-production-patterns/' | relative_url }}).
